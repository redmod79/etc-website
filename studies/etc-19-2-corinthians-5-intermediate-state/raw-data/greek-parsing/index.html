    Word Lemma Strong's Parsing
    dei dei G1163 Pres Act Ind 3S "it is necessary"
    to phtharton phthartos G5349 Adj Nom Sg N "the corruptible"
    touto houtos G3778 Dem Nom Sg N "this"
    endysasthai endyO G1746 Aor Mid Inf "to clothe itself"

    1 Cor 15:54¶

    Greek: hotan de to phtharton touto endysEtai aphtharsian kai to thnEton touto endysEtai athanasian, tote genEsetai ho logos ho gegrammenos KatepothE ho thanatos eis nikos.

    Key: KatepothE (G2666) = Aor Pass Ind "was swallowed up" -- same root as katapothE in 2 Cor 5:4 (Aor Pass Subj)

    Rom 8:22-23¶

    Greek 8:22: oidamen gar hoti pasa hE ktisis systenazei kai synOdinei achri tou nyn; Greek 8:23: ou monon de, alla kai autoi tEn aparchEn tou Pneumatos echontes hEmeis kai autoi en heautois stenazomen, huiothesian apekdechomenoi, tEn apolytrOsin tou sOmatos hEmOn.

    Key: stenazomen (G4727) = Pres Act Ind 1P "we groan" -- same word as 2 Cor 5:2,4 apekdechomenoi = Pres M/P Ptcp "awaiting eagerly" apolytrOsin tou sOmatos = "the redemption of the body"

    Php 1:21-24¶

    Greek 1:21: Emoi gar to zEn Christos kai to apothanein kerdos. Greek 1:23: synechomai de ek tOn duo, tEn epithymian echOn eis to analysai kai syn ChristO einai, pollO gar mallon kreisson;

    Key: analysai (G360) = Aor Act Inf "to depart"; einai = Pres Act Inf "to be" Note: Paul uses analysai (to depart) not ekdEmEsai (to be absent from home)

    2 Pe 1:13-14¶

    Greek 1:13: dikaion de hEgoumai, eph' hoson eimi en toutO tO skEnOmati, diegeirein hymas en hypomnEsei, Greek 1:14: eidOs hoti tachinE estin hE apothesis tou skEnOmatos mou, kathOs kai ho Kyrios hEmOn IEsous Christos edElOsen moi;

    Key: skEnOmati (G4638) = Noun Dat Sg N "tabernacle" -- related to skEnos (G4636) in 2 Cor 5:1,4 apothesis (G595) = Noun Nom Sg F "putting off/laying aside"

    Textual Variants (N1904 vs TR)¶

    Chapter-level summary: 2CO 5¶

    21/21 verses show variants (mostly spelling/article differences)

    Verse-level comparisons:¶

    2 Cor 5:1: N1904: oIdamen gar hoti ean hE epigeios hEmOn oikia tou skEnous kataluthE, oikodomEn ek Theou echomen, oikian acheiropoiEton aiOnion en tois ouranois. TR: [word count differs by 3; Strong's in N1904 only: G1437, G2192, G2647, G1492; Strong's in TR only: G5758, G5686, G5719] Note: Differences appear to be parsing codes not substantive word changes.

    2 Cor 5:3 (CRITICAL VARIANT): N1904: ei ge kai endysamenoi ou gymnoi heurethEsometha. (7 words) TR: eige kai ou gymnoi (4 words) N1904 has: G1487 (ei), G1065 (ge), G1746 (endyO), G2147 (heuriskO) -- not in TR TR has: G1489 (eige compound) -- not in N1904 Note: TR appears shorter; N1904 has the full "having been clothed" (endysamenoi) and "shall be found" (heurethEsometha). This is a significant variant as some MSS read ekdysamenoi ("having been UNclothed") instead of endysamenoi ("having been clothed"). This variant changes the meaning substantially.

    2 Cor 5:4: N1904: 23 words; TR: 21 words Differences appear to be parsing/morphology codes rather than substantive word changes.

    2 Cor 5:8: N1904: 14 words; TR: 12 words Both contain the key vocabulary (ekdEmEsai, endEmEsai). Differences are minor.
