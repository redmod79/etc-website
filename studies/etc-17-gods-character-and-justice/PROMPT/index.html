    Bible Study: God's Character and the Consistency of Justice¶

    Question¶

    God's love, justice, holiness, mercy, wrath. Proportionality of punishment. Consistency of infinite torment vs. destruction with biblical justice.

    Verse References (from Nave's entries)¶

    A. God's Self-Revelation / Character Statements - Exo 34:6-7 (God proclaims name: merciful, gracious, longsuffering, abundant in goodness/truth, forgiving, yet will not clear the guilty) - Deut 32:4 (God's work perfect, all ways judgment, God of truth, just and right) - Ps 103:8-14 (merciful, gracious, slow to anger, does not always chide, knows our frame/dust) - Ps 145:8-9, 17 (gracious, full of compassion, slow to anger, great in mercy, tender mercies over all works; righteous in all ways, holy in all works) - Nah 1:2-3 (God is jealous, revengeth, furious; slow to anger, great in power, will not at all acquit the wicked) - Mic 7:18 (who is a God like thee that pardons iniquity, does not retain anger forever, delights in mercy) - Lam 3:22-23 (LORD's mercies never consumed, compassions fail not) - Isa 55:7-9 (God will abundantly pardon, thoughts/ways higher)

    B. Proportional Punishment Passages - Luke 12:47-48 (beaten with many stripes vs. few stripes, according to knowledge) - Matt 10:15; 11:22,24 (more tolerable for Sodom than for rejecting cities) - Matt 23:14 (greater damnation/krisis) - Heb 2:2-3 (every transgression received just recompense; how much more) - Heb 10:28-29 (sorer punishment for treading underfoot Son of God) - Rom 2:5-6 (treasurest up wrath; render to every man according to deeds) - 2 Cor 5:10 (receive according to what done in body) - Col 3:25 (he that doeth wrong shall receive for the wrong) - Job 34:11 (work of a man shall he render unto him) - Ps 62:12 (thou renderest to every man according to his work) - Isa 3:10-11 (say to righteous well; woe unto wicked, reward of his hands) - Ezek 18:19-20,25-30 (soul that sins shall die; God's way equal) - Jer 17:10 (give according to fruit of doings) - Prov 24:12 (render to every man according to his works)

    C. Biblical Punishment Patterns (Historical Judgments) - Gen 6:7; 7:21-23 (the Flood -- all flesh destroyed) - Gen 19:24-25 (Sodom and Gomorrah -- fire and brimstone, overthrew) - Num 16:31-35 (Korah's rebellion -- earth swallowed, fire consumed) - Num 14:26-39 (40 years wandering, those who murmured died in wilderness) - Lev 10:1-2 (Nadab and Abihu -- fire devoured) - Exo 32:27-28,35 (golden calf judgment) - Josh 7:15-25 (Achan -- burned with fire) - 2 Kings 19:35 (Sennacherib's army -- 185,000 destroyed) - Gen 3:17-19 (Adam's sentence -- death/return to dust) - Gen 4:11-15 (Cain -- cursed from the earth but NOT killed)

    D. "Wages of Sin" / Death as Penalty - Rom 6:23 (wages of sin is death; gift of God is eternal life) - Ezek 18:4,20 (soul that sins shall die) - Gen 2:17 (in the day thou eatest thou shalt surely die) - James 1:15 (sin when finished brings forth death) - Rom 5:12 (death passed upon all men)

    E. God's Desire for Salvation / Not Delighting in Death - 2 Pet 3:9 (not willing that any should perish, but all come to repentance) - Ezek 18:23,32; 33:11 (no pleasure in death of wicked; turn and live) - 1 Tim 2:4 (God will have all men to be saved) - John 3:16-17 (God so loved the world; not to condemn but save)

    F. Key Destruction/Death Vocabulary (from prior studies) - Matt 10:28 (destroy soul and body in gehenna) - 2 Thess 1:9 (everlasting destruction) - John 3:16 (perish vs. eternal life) - Mal 4:1-3 (burn up, ashes underfoot) - Ps 37:20 (wicked perish, consume into smoke) - Rev 20:14 (lake of fire = second death)

    Focus Areas¶

    1. God's self-revealed character -- What does God say about Himself? (Ex 34:6-7; Ps 103; 145; Mic 7:18; Lam 3:22-23)
    2. Biblical proportionality principle -- Luke 12:47-48 (degrees of punishment); Matt 10:15, 11:22 (more tolerable); Heb 2:2-3 (just recompense)
    3. Historical punishment patterns -- Every recorded divine judgment: result = death/destruction, NOT eternal torment (flood, Sodom, Korah, Nadab/Abihu)
    4. "According to deeds" passages -- Rom 2:5-6; 2 Cor 5:10; Col 3:25; Ezek 7:3,27; Job 34:11; Ps 62:12
    5. God's stated desire -- Not willing any perish (2 Pet 3:9); no pleasure in death of wicked (Ezek 18:23,32; 33:11)
    6. Death as the stated penalty -- Rom 6:23; Ezek 18:4,20; Gen 2:17; James 1:15
    7. Consistency test -- Does infinite torment for finite sin match the "according to deeds" principle? Does destruction?
