# Bible Study: The Lake of Fire and the Second Death

## Question

What is the "lake of fire" in Revelation? What is the "second death"? Who are cast into the lake of fire? What does the "second death" language tell us about the nature of the final fate of the wicked? Is the lake of fire a place of ongoing conscious torment, or does the "second death" describe final destruction/annihilation?

### Focus Areas

1. **All five lake-of-fire passages** — systematic comparison of subjects, language, and vocabulary used for each group cast in
2. **"Second death" = death language** — what does "death" (G2288 thanatos, H4194 mavet) mean throughout Scripture? Does the name "second death" carry the meaning of death or of torment?
3. **Death as last enemy** — 1 Cor 15:24-28,54-57: if death is the last enemy to be "destroyed" (katargeo), and death and hades are cast into the lake = second death, what is the relationship?
4. **Death and Hades as personified powers** — Rev 1:18; 6:8; 20:13-14: Christ holds their keys; they are eventually cast into the lake. The abolition of death = the lake of fire
5. **"No more death" in the new creation** — Rev 21:4 promises no more death. If the second death is ongoing eternal conscious torment, how does this relate to "no more death"?
6. **Overcomer exemption** — Rev 2:11; 20:6: "second death has no power" over overcomers — what is exempted from? Torment? Death? Both?
7. **Fire and brimstone as destruction vs. ongoing torment** — Sodom as type (Gen 19:24; 2 Pet 2:6 = ashes; Jude 7 = eternal fire); Isa 30:33 (Topheth); Isa 34:10 (smoke for ever, yet desolate)
8. **The antithesis structure** — inheritance (life) vs. lake (second death) as parallel to Jn 3:16 (perish vs. eternal life) and Rom 6:23 (death vs. eternal life)
9. **Deduplication** — check N021, E110, E113, IA013 before adding (all in master already)
