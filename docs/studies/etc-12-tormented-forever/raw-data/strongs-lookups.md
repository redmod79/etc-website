**All NT occurrences:**
- Matthew 25:46 — "everlasting punishment" (aionion kolasin)

## G166 — αἰώνιος (aiōnios)

**Transliteration:** aiṓnios
**Part of Speech:** adjective
**BLB Count:** 71
**Definition:** from G165; perpetual (past, present, and future as well) — eternal, for ever, everlasting, world

**Key judgment occurrences:**
- Matthew 25:41 — "everlasting fire" (πῦρ τὸ αἰώνιον)
- Matthew 25:46 — "everlasting punishment" (κόλασιν αἰώνιον) and "life eternal" (ζωὴν αἰώνιον)
- Mark 3:29 — "eternal damnation" (aioniou kriseōs)
- 2 Thessalonians 1:9 — "everlasting destruction" (olethron aionion)
- Hebrews 6:2 — "eternal judgment"
- Revelation 14:6 — "everlasting gospel"

**Parsing in Mat 25:46:** Both instances are [A-ASF] Accusative Singular Feminine — grammatically identical, modifying the same syntactic position. "Everlasting punishment" and "life eternal" use the same grammatical form of G166.
