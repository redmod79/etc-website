## Genesis 3:22

Hebrew text: וַיֹּ֣אמֶר׀ יְהוָ֣ה אֱלֹהִ֗ים הֵ֤ן הָֽאָדָם֙ הָיָה֙ כְּאַחַ֣ד מִמֶּ֔נּוּ לָדַ֖עַת טֹ֣וב וָרָ֑ע וְעַתָּ֣ה׀ פֶּן־יִשְׁלַ֣ח יָדֹ֗ו וְלָקַח֙ גַּ֚ם מֵעֵ֣ץ הַֽחַיִּ֔ים וְאָכַ֖ל וָחַ֥י לְעֹלָֽם׃

WORD ANALYSIS:
  וַיֹּ֣אמֶר = and say (Verb.Qal.Wayq.3ms) — wayyiqtol = narrative main clause
  יְהוָ֣ה = YHWH (Proper Noun)
  אֱלֹהִ֗ים = god(s) (Noun.mp.Abs)
  הֵ֤ן = behold (Interjection)
  הָֽאָדָם֙ = the human, mankind (Noun.ms.Abs with article)
  הָיָה֙ = be (Verb.Qal.Perf.3ms)
  כְּאַחַ֣ד = as one (Prep + Noun) — "become as one of us"
  מִמֶּ֔נּוּ = from (Prep + 1 common plural suffix = "from us")
  לָדַ֖עַת = to know (Verb.Qal.InfCon.Cst — infinitive construct)
  טֹ֣וב = good (Adj.ms.Abs)
  וָרָ֑ע = and evil (Conj + Adj.ms.Abs)
  וְעַתָּ֣ה = and now (Conj + Adverb)
  פֶּן = lest (Conjunction of fear)
  יִשְׁלַ֣ח = send (Verb.Qal.Impf.3ms) — imperfect = potential/feared action
  יָדֹ֗ו = his hand (Noun.s.Abs + 3ms suffix)
  וְלָקַח֙ = and take (Conj + Verb.Qal.Perf.3ms)
  גַּ֚ם = even, also (Adverb)
  מֵעֵ֣ץ = from tree (Prep + Noun.ms.Cst)
  הַחַיִּ֔ים = the life (Art + Noun.mp.Abs — H2416 chayyim, plural of intensity = "life" in fullness)
  וְאָכַ֖ל = and eat (Conj + Verb.Qal.Perf.3ms)
  וָחַ֥י = and live/be alive (Conj + Verb.Qal.Perf.3ms — H2416/H2421 chay)
  לְעֹלָֽם = to eternity/forever (Prep + Noun.ms.Abs — H5769 olam)

KEY PHRASE: "וָחַי לְעֹלָם" = "and live for ever"
- The full phrase: "lest he... take also of the tree of life, and eat, AND LIVE FOR EVER"
- Structure: God's fear (expressed as lest-clause) = man acquiring endless life from external source
- chay le-olam = literally "be-alive to-eternity" — the exact thing God prevents
- This is the foundational OT text for the concept: man does NOT inherently possess immortality; it would require access to the tree of life

## Daniel 12:2

Hebrew text: וְרַבִּ֕ים מִיְּשֵׁנֵ֥י אַדְמַת־עָפָ֖ר יָקִ֑יצוּ אֵ֚לֶּה לְחַיֵּ֣י עֹולָ֔ם וְאֵ֥לֶּה לַחֲרָפֹ֖ות לְדִרְאֹ֥ון עֹולָֽם׃

WORD ANALYSIS:
  וְרַבִּ֕ים = and many (Conj + Adj.mp.Abs — "many" as subject)
  מִיְּשֵׁנֵ֥י = from sleeping (Prep + Adj.mp.Cst — ישׁן yashen = sleeping ones)
  אַדְמַת = soil (Noun.fs.Cst — adamat = of the ground/soil)
  עָפָ֖ר = dust (Noun.ms.Abs — aphar = dust)
  יָקִ֑יצוּ = awake/arise (Verb.Hiphil.Impf.3mp — קיץ qits = to awake; Hiphil = causative = "shall be caused to awake" or "shall awake")
  אֵ֚לֶּה = these (Demonstrative Pronoun.p)
  לְחַיֵּ֣י = to life (Prep + Noun.mp.Cst — chayyei, from H2416/H2421)
  עֹולָ֔ם = eternity (Noun.ms.Abs — H5769 olam)
  וְאֵ֥לֶּה = and these (Conj + Demonstrative)
  לַחֲרָפֹ֖ות = to reproaches/shame (Prep + Noun.fp.Abs — חרפה cherpah)
  לְדִרְאֹ֥ון = to abhorrence (Prep + Noun.ms.Cst — דראון deraon = contempt, aversion)
  עֹולָֽם = eternity (Noun.ms.Abs — H5769 olam)

KEY PHRASES:
  "chayyei olam" (חַיֵּי עֹולָם) = "life of eternity / everlasting life" — H2416+H5769
  "direaon olam" (דִרְאֹון עֹולָם) = "everlasting contempt/abhorrence" — rare word, also in Isaiah 66:24

NOTES:
- The sleeping ones are "from the dust of the ground" (adamat-aphar) — echoes Gen 3:19; the dead are in the ground
- The awakening is to two different destinies: chayyei olam (everlasting life) vs. direaon olam (everlasting contempt)
- This is the clearest OT text anticipating a general resurrection and two-destiny outcome
- "Chayyei olam" is the Hebrew equivalent that the LXX translates as "zoe aionios" (eternal life)
