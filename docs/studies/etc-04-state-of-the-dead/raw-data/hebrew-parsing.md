### Grammatical Notes
- **הַמֵּתִ֞ים** (hammethim): "the dead ones" — a Qal participle of מות (muth, to die) used as a noun. It simply means "those who are dead."
- **אֵינָ֧ם יֹודְעִ֣ים מְא֗וּמָה**: "they are not knowing anything" — present participle with the negation particle אין (ein). The construction אינם + participle = strong present negation: "they are not (in the state of) knowing anything." This is not a past tense or occasional negation — it is a statement of ongoing condition.
- **מְאוּמָה** (meumah): "anything/something" — with אין = absolute negation. "Not anything" = nothing whatsoever.
- **אֵין עוֹד**: "there is no more" — duration word עוֹד (od) with negation = "never again / no longer."
- **שָׂכָר** (sakar): "reward, hire, wage" — "they have no more reward" = their consciousness and engagement in rewardable activity has ended.
- **נִשְׁכַּח זִכְרָם** (nishkach zikhram): "their memory is forgotten" — Niphal perfect (passive), the memory of them has been forgotten.

### Key Observation
The Hebrew grammar provides no support for the "under-the-sun" qualifier: v.5 says "the dead know not any thing" (unqualified), while v.6 then adds the "under the sun" qualifier specifically for their lack of portion in earthly activity. The two clauses have different scope: v.5 addresses knowledge/consciousness; v.6 addresses participation in earthly affairs. The "under the sun" restriction in v.6 does not retroactively qualify v.5's unqualified statement.

### Grammatical Notes
- **רוּחֹו** (rucho): "his spirit/breath/wind" — ruach (H7307) with 3ms pronominal suffix. The same ruach that is shared by man and beast in Ecc 3:19-21; the same ruach that returns to God in Ecc 12:7. The possessive "his" does not specify what aspect of the person returns — the context (v.3: "in whom there is no help") frames this as the dissolution of the person.
- **יָשֻׁב לְאַדְמָתֹו** (he returns to his ground): Qal imperfect of שׁוב (shub, to return). The "ground" is the earth from which man was made (Gen 2:7; 3:19). The parallel with Ecc 12:7 ("the dust returns to the earth") is exact.
- **בַּיֹּום הַהוּא** (on that very day): a strong temporal locator — the very day of death. This is the critical timing marker: it is on the day of dying (not later, not gradually) that the thoughts perish.
- **אָבְדוּ עֶשְׁתֹּנֹתָיו** (his thoughts perish): Qal perfect 3pl of אָבַד (abad, to perish/be lost). The Qal perfect is used here as a gnomic/proverbial perfect, describing what happens at the moment of death as a settled reality. **עֶשְׁתֹּנֹת** (eshtono): "thoughts, plans, purposes" — the active mental/cognitive content of the person. These perish on the day of death.

### Key Observation
The combination of v.4's clauses: (1) the ruach goes out (stops animating), (2) he returns to the earth, (3) ON THAT VERY DAY his thoughts perish. The timing marker "that very day" forecloses any gradual transition. This is the strongest explicit temporal statement in Scripture on the moment-of-death cessation of consciousness.

### Grammatical Notes
- **מִיְּשֵׁנֵי** (mi-yshenei): "from the sleepers of" — the preposition מן + construct form of ישׁן (H3462, yashen: to sleep). The sleeping ones are in "the dust of the earth" (admat-aphar). The construction joins two nouns: the sleep-place is specified as "ground-dust."
- **יְּשֵׁנֵי** (construct of yashen): the adjective form used as a noun — "sleepers." This is H3462 in construct plural, directly connected to "ground of dust." The dead are defined as sleepers in the dust.
- **יָקִיצוּ** (yaqitsu): Hiphil imperfect of קִיץ (qits, H6974: to awake). The Hiphil of qits means "to cause to awake" or "to awake" (intransitive Hiphil). The awaiting is active — they will awake.
- **עוֹלָם** (olam): "eternity/age" — used twice. The awakening leads to either eternal life or eternal contempt. The OT has no clearer statement of resurrection to two outcomes than this verse.

### Key Observation
Dan 12:2 uses H3462 (yashen) for the dead — describing their current state as sleep ("sleepers of the dust"). The resurrection is described as waking from this sleep. The sleep = their current state; the waking = the resurrection event. There is no intermediate waking described between the sleep of death and the waking of resurrection.
