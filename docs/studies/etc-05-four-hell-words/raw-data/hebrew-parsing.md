KEY GRAMMATICAL NOTES FOR ISA 66:24:
1. פִגְרֵי (pigrê) = CORPSES (plural construct of פֶּגֶר, peger)
   - The objects being seen are CORPSES — DEAD BODIES, not conscious persons

2. תֹולַעְתָּ֞ם לֹ֣א תָמ֗וּת = "their worm does not die"
   - The worm feeding on corpses is what is unceasing
   - Maggots/decay agents continue their work — the body is fully consumed
3. אִשָּׁם֙ לֹ֣א תִכְבֶּ֔ה = "their fire is not quenched"
   - The fire burning is not put out prematurely — it burns until the work is done
   - Mark 9:48 quotes this exactly: "their worm dieth not, and the fire is not quenched"
4. דֵרָאֹ֖ון (deraon) = abhorrence/repulsion — hapax in Isaiah
   - Also in Daniel 12:2 ("everlasting contempt")
   - The outcome is contempt/loathing for the dead rebels
