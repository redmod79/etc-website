## Key Findings from Each Prior Study

### etc5-01: What Is Man?
- Man was formed from dust + breath of life; became a living soul (nephesh chayyah) -- identical formula applies to animals (Gen 1:24; 2:7); soul is the whole person, not a separable part
- God alone possesses immortality (athanasia, 1 Tim 6:16); humans are mortal (thnetos); soul can die (Ezek 18:4); nephesh can refer to a corpse
- At death, dust returns to earth, spirit returns to God, thoughts perish (Psa 146:4); the dead know nothing (Ecc 9:5); death = sleep consistently across authors and testaments

### etc5-02: Who Has Immortality?
- Immortality vocabulary survey (athanasia, aphtharsia, aphthartos): zero NT occurrences apply to humans as presently possessing immortality; all 18 NT immortality-term occurrences either ascribe immortality to God alone or place it at resurrection
- Tree of life = external, mediated immortality; barred after the fall to prevent humans living forever in a sinful state; required for ongoing life
- "Immortal soul" phrase does not appear anywhere in Scripture; immortality is "sought for" (Rom 2:7) and "put on" (1 Cor 15:53-54) at resurrection, not innately possessed

### etc5-03: Biblical Death
- Three death registers identified: physical death, spiritual alienation, eschatological second death; apollumi (G622) is the primary NT word for eschatological destruction
- Second death = lake of fire (N021; Rev 20:14); death and life are presented as opposite outcomes, not two modes of eternal existence; death is "the last enemy" to be "destroyed" (1 Cor 15:26)
- Rich Man and Lazarus classified IB006 (Unresolved at this stage); remedy for death is consistently resurrection, not release of a pre-existing immortal soul

### etc5-04: State of the Dead
- IB001 shifted from Unresolved to Moderate AGAINST conscious intermediate state; SIS #4a connections established: 1 Cor 15:18 (Paul's "dead in Christ perished" context) governs Phil 1:23; Acts 2:31 (hades = Christ's grave) governs Luk 23:43
- N025 established: Rev 20:5 ("but the rest of the dead lived not again until...") frames the dead as "not living" -- the text's own framework; N026 established: Hades = temporary holding state, distinct from lake of fire
- Didactic passages (Psalms, Ecclesiastes, Job) consistently state the dead lack consciousness; figurative/poetic passages (1 Sam 28, Rev 6:9-11) must be read in light of the didactic

### etc5-05: Four Hell Words
- Sheol (H7585) and hades (G86) are the same concept (LXX translates sheol as hades consistently); both are common destination of ALL the dead, righteous and wicked alike; hades is cast INTO the lake of fire (Rev 20:14), not the lake of fire itself
- Torment vocabulary (basanizo/basanismos) is entirely absent from all 12 gehenna passages; gehenna uses apollumi (destroy), not basanizo (torment); the Sodom paradigm ("eternal fire" = ashes; 2 Pet 2:6; Jude 7) is Scripture's own designated example
- E149 (Rev 20:10) and E150 (Rev 14:10-11) are the two genuine ECT E-items from Revelation torment passages; IB008 (Moderate against universal extension of Rev 14:10-11 to all wicked) established

### etc5-06: Destruction Vocabulary
- 15+ Hebrew/Greek destruction word families with ~2,060+ occurrences; not one of these words is ever translated "torment"; destruction and torment vocabularies are lexically non-overlapping
- LXX bridge confirmed: H6 (abad) → G622 (apollumi), PMI 6.14; non-existence expressions established (Psa 37 cluster; Obad 1:16 "as though they had not been"); Isa 66:24 = corpses, not living beings
- Every destruction image (chaff, stubble, wax, ashes, smoke, consumed) depicts cessation of existence, not ongoing torment; didactic passages (Paul's epistles, Synoptics outside Revelation) consistently use destruction vocabulary

### etc5-07: Olam/Forever in OT
- H5769 olam: contextually determined duration; does NOT inherently mean "infinite" (applied to slave's service, Jonah's 3 days in the fish, hills that scatter) and does NOT inherently mean "finite" (applied to God's existence)
- Hab 3:6 same-verse demonstration: olam hills are shattered (finite), olam ways of God endure (infinite) -- both uses in the same verse confirm contextual determination
- Dan 12:2: identical grammatical construction (olam) applied to both "everlasting life" and "shame and everlasting contempt" -- the actual duration for each must be determined by the nature of the referent, not the word alone; Jer 51 "perpetual sleep / shall not wake" as OT parallel for final destruction

### etc5-08: Aionios in NT
- G166 aionios: a denominal adjective (aion + ios = "age-pertaining"), not inherently endless; past-time uses confirmed (Rom 16:25; 2Tim 1:9; Tit 1:2 -- all refer to past ages, not infinite past); aidios (G126, from aei = "always") carries inherent endlessness; Paul chose aidios for God's power (Rom 1:20) and used aionios differently
- Aionios + event-noun pattern: "eternal redemption" (Heb 9:12), "eternal salvation" (Heb 5:9), "eternal judgment" (Heb 6:2) -- all denote permanent results of completed acts, not ongoing processes; kolasis in Mat 25:46 is punishment/pruning (G2851, from kolazo = prune/curtail), not basanismos (torment)
- IB012 established (Moderate toward referent-nature): the same adjective (aionios) in the same form in Mat 25:46 for both "punishment" and "life" means the actual duration of each is determined by the nature of the referent -- if life is endless, life determines aionios as endless; if punishment is destruction/cessation, destruction determines aionios as permanent result

### etc5-09: Rich Man and Lazarus
- IB006 resolved from Unresolved to Moderate (toward parabolic/literary genre): the "certain rich man" opening formula (tis plousios) in Lk 12:16 is explicitly labeled a parable by Luke; the identical formula in Lk 16:1 and 16:19 falls within the same continuous parable discourse (Lk 15:1-16:31)
- Even on a literal reading, Lk 16 cannot establish ECT: (a) the setting is hades, not the lake of fire (per N026, hades is temporary); (b) hades is cast INTO the lake of fire at Rev 20:14; (c) the stated purpose is "Moses and the prophets" sufficiency, not eschatology
- E+N tier positional tally after etc5-09: Conditionalist 135, ECT 8, Neutral 125 (total 268 E+N items)

### etc5-10: Souls Under the Altar
- IB013 RESOLVED STRONG toward personification/blood-testimony interpretation: the blood-crying convention from Gen 4:10 (Abel's blood cries from the ground) is confirmed as the dominant OT parallel; Rev 6:10 follows this convention; the altar itself speaks in Rev 16:7 (personification within Revelation)
- E051 reclassified from ECT to Neutral: Rev 6:9-11 identifies subjects (souls of martyrs, sacrificial altar position, blood-pouring location) without establishing their consciousness -- a textual observation both sides must accept
- Martyrs "live" at first resurrection per Rev 20:4-5; "the rest of the dead lived not again until the thousand years were finished" -- the phrase "lived not" in Rev 20:5 matches the framework established in N025

### etc5-11: Smoke Ascending Forever
- IB009 upgraded from Moderate to STRONG (toward permanent desolation, not ongoing torment): Rev 19:3 contains the identical smoke-ascending formula for Babylon, which is described as "utterly burned with fire" and "found no more" -- a within-Revelation structural parallel demonstrating the formula describes permanent aftermath of completed destruction
- Isa 34:10 (Edom's judgment: "smoke shall go up forever"; "none shall pass through it for ever and ever") is the highest OT parallel, where animals inhabit the desolated land = permanent desolation result, not perpetual combustion
- IB014 established (Moderate toward judgment-process): "no rest day and night" appears in Rev 4:8 for the living creatures in uninterrupted worship -- same construction used in a non-torment context; the phrase describes the judgment period, not eternal ongoing torment

### etc5-12: Tormented Forever
- Rev 20:10 applies the basanisthēsontai (torment) formula to exactly three named entities: devil, beast, false prophet; the beast is a symbolic kingdom-entity; the false prophet is a symbolic power-system; at minimum two of three subjects of the "torment forever" formula are symbolic, non-individual entities
- Rev 20:15 casts human wicked "into the lake of fire" without repeating the torment formula; the lake of fire when applied to humans is defined as "the second death" (thanatos = standard Greek death word), not "tormented forever"
- IB015 established (Moderate, toward permanent/irrevocable): the "forever" formula on Rev 20:10 is genuine but IB009 (Strong for smoke-formula = permanent desolation) prevents a Strong ECT resolution; the formula indicates permanence/irrevocability, not necessarily ongoing conscious experience

### etc5-13: Lake of Fire / Second Death
- Second death = thanatos (G2288, standard Greek death word, not basanismos); Rev 20:14 equates the lake of fire with "the second death" and Rev 21:8 repeats the equation; five lake-of-fire passages with four distinct subject groups: (1) beast/false prophet, (2) death and hades, (3) human wicked, (4) devil
- Three distinct vocabulary sets for three groups in Rev 20:9-15: "devoured" (fire from heaven, Rev 20:9) for the surrounding armies; "tormented day and night for ever" (Rev 20:10) for the three named supernatural entities; "second death" (Rev 20:14-15) for the human wicked
- Death and Hades are cast into the lake of fire as personified powers (N042: Rev 20:14 + 1 Cor 15:26 = abolition of Death; result = Rev 21:4 no more death); Death is destroyed, not tormented

### etc5-14: Judgment Passages
- All 10 major judgment passages examined (Mat 25:46; Mat 10:28; Mk 9:43-48; 2 Thess 1:9; Dan 12:2; Rev 20:12; Acts 24:15; Jhn 5:29; Rom 2:8-12; Heb 10:27-31) consistently use judicial/forensic vocabulary (krinō, krisis, G2920) and destruction vocabulary (apollumi, esthiein, diaphtheirō), NOT torment vocabulary (basanizo)
- N044 established: apollumi is Paul's summary destruction word in Rom 2:12 ("they that have sinned without law shall also perish without law" = apollōntai); N046 established: Heb 10:27 esthiein = consumption vocabulary, not torment vocabulary
- IB016 (Mat 25:41 -- fire prepared for devil and angels; Moderate against universal human extension) and IB017 (Jhn 5:29 krisis vocabulary; Moderate against torment-reading of krisis) established; IA023 and IA024 established: vocabulary distribution shows destruction vocabulary in ALL non-specific passages, torment vocabulary only in Revelation

### etc5-15: ECT Strongest Case
- CRITICAL FINDING: Zero ECT arguments reach the N (Necessary Implication) tier for the universal claim that ALL human wicked experience ongoing conscious torment FOREVER; all 8 ECT text-arguments examined remain at I-B (Competing Evidence) tier
- The 7 ECT E-items confirmed (E038, E039, E050, E052, E123, E149, E150); none yield N-tier support for the universal claim; E038/E039/E050/E052 address the intermediate state (righteous); E123/E149/E150 address Revelation torment passages without establishing universal extension

### etc5-16: Origins of ECT
- Historical ECT origins classified as I-C/I-D tier: Greek Tartaros mythology context (E284-E285), Plato's immortal soul doctrine from Phaedo (E286), and the NT's own restricted use of tartaroo (2 Pet 2:4 -- applied only to fallen angels, not humans; IC003)
- Kolasis (G2851) etymology: from kolazo (to prune, curtail, restrain); original meaning is pruning/curtailing, not ongoing torment; IA026 established: torment vocabulary (basanizo, basanismos) is confined to Revelation's apocalyptic imagery, while destruction vocabulary pervades the NT didactic teaching -- Conditionalist I-A
- Nave's Topical Bible organizes the final fate of the wicked under "DESTRUCTION of the wicked," not "torment" or "eternal conscious suffering" -- reflecting the historic topical-Bible classification of the dominant biblical vocabulary

### etc5-17: God's Character and Justice
- Five passages state God does NOT will/take pleasure in the apollumi (destruction) or death of the wicked: Ezek 18:23,32; Ezek 33:11; 2 Pet 3:9; 1 Tim 2:4; Lam 3:33; these are classified as Conditionalist E-items because they connect God's non-desire with the apollumi/death vocabulary that describes the wicked's fate

### Cross-Study Patterns to Examine (REQUIRED)

**Pattern 1: The Mortality Foundation**
Studies 01-04 established that humans are mortal, lack inherent immortality, and the intermediate dead are described as unconscious in didactic passages. How does this foundation affect interpretation of every subsequent passage about the fate of the wicked?

**Pattern 2: The Vocabulary Divide**
Study 06 established that destruction vocabulary (~2,060+ occurrences) and torment vocabulary (~22 occurrences) are non-overlapping. Studies 05, 11, 12, 14 confirmed: torment vocabulary is absent from all 12 gehenna passages, from Paul's epistles, from the Synoptic judgment passages, and from 9 of 10 major judgment passages. How does this consistent distribution pattern affect the weight of evidence?

**Pattern 3: The Didactic vs. Apocalyptic Pattern**
Multiple studies found that didactic/teaching passages use destruction vocabulary while torment vocabulary is concentrated in Revelation's apocalyptic imagery. What does this genre distribution establish about which vocabulary set represents the direct teaching of Scripture?

**Pattern 4: The "Forever" Resolution**
Studies 07-08 established that olam and aionios are subject-determined, not duration-imposing. Aidios carries inherent endlessness; Paul chose aidios for God's power and aionios for punishment language. How does this resolve every "everlasting fire" and "eternal punishment" passage?

**Pattern 5: The ECT Proof-Text Pattern**
Studies 09-12 examined the four major claimed ECT proof-texts: Rich Man and Lazarus (IB006 Moderate parabolic), Souls Under the Altar (IB013 Strong personification), Smoke Ascending Forever (IB009 Strong desolation), Tormented Forever (IB015 Moderate permanent/irrevocable). What happened when each was examined in context? What does the pattern of I-B resolution indicate?

**Pattern 6: The Three-Vocabulary Pattern of Rev 20:9-15**
Studies 11, 12, 13, 14, 15 consistently identified three distinct vocabulary sets for three groups in Rev 20:9-15: (1) "devoured" for surrounding armies, (2) "tormented day and night for ever" for three named supernatural entities, (3) "second death" (thanatos) for human wicked. What is the significance of this consistent finding?

**Pattern 7: The Zero-N-Tier Finding**
Study 15 found zero ECT arguments reaching the N tier for the universal claim. What does it mean that the strongest case for ECT cannot generate any Necessary Implications for the claim that ALL human wicked experience ongoing conscious torment forever?

**Pattern 8: The I-B Resolution Direction**
All 18 I-B items: 2 Strong (both conditionalist), 15 Moderate (all conditionalist direction or against ECT), 1 Unresolved. What does this systematic pattern indicate about the weight of competing evidence?

**Pattern 9: The Sodom Paradigm**
Scripture's own designated example of "eternal fire" (Sodom, Jude 7) was "reduced to ashes" (2 Pet 2:6); Jesus said fire "destroyed them all" (Luk 17:29). This appeared in studies 05, 06, 11, 13, 15. How does this paradigm govern interpretation of "eternal fire" language throughout Scripture?

**Pattern 10: The New Creation**
Studies 11, 13, 15, 17 all examined Rev 21:4 -- "no more death, sorrow, crying, pain." Death itself is destroyed (1 Cor 15:26; Rev 20:14). If the wicked were in eternal conscious torment, death would not be destroyed and there would remain ongoing suffering. How does the new creation picture relate to the fate of the wicked?

**Pattern 11: The Character Argument**
Study 17 established that God does not will the apollumi of any; God's anger is non-perpetual; graduated punishment applies. How does the character evidence (I-B tier, Moderate conditionalist) interact with the vocabulary evidence (E/N tier, Conditionalist) and the anthropology evidence (E/N tier, Conditionalist)?
