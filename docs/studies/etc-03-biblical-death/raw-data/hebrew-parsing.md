### Grammatical Notes — Gen 2:17

The critical phrase is: מֹ֥ות תָּמֽוּת (moth tamuth)
- מֹות = Infinitive Absolute of מות (muwth, H4191)
- תָּמוּת = Qal Imperfect 2ms of מות (muwth, H4191)

The Hebrew infinitive absolute + imperfect construction is an emphatic idiom.
The standard Hebrew grammar: "dying you shall die" or "you shall surely die."
This is one of the strongest possible affirmations in Biblical Hebrew.
The same construction appears in Gen 2:17 ("moth tamuth") and is echoed in
the serpent's negation in Gen 3:4 ("lo moth tamuth" — "you shall not surely die").

### Grammatical Notes — Ezek 18:4

Key clause: הַנֶּפֶשׁ הַחֹטֵאת הִיא תָמוּת
- הַנֶּפֶשׁ = "the soul" (with article)

- הִיא = emphatic 3fs personal pronoun "she/it"
- תָמוּת = Qal Impf 3fs "shall die"

The pronoun הִיא (hi') inserted between subject and verb is emphatic:
"the soul — IT — shall die." The pronoun makes the predicate emphatic.
nephesh (soul) here is the subject of muwth (die), meaning the soul itself
is what dies; it is not immortal by nature.
The verse occurs 3 times in Ezek 18 (vv. 4, 13, 20) as refrain.

### Grammatical Notes — Isa 25:8

Key phrase: בִּלַּע הַמָּוֶת לָנֶצַח
- בִּלַּע = Piel Perfect 3ms of בלע (bala') = "he has swallowed up"

in victory." The Hebrew Piel perfect can be prophetic perfect (certainty of
future action expressed as already accomplished).
