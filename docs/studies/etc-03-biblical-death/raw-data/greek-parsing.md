### Grammatical Notes — ROM 6:23

Structure: Two parallel clauses with implied copula (no verb "is/are"):
1. τὰ ὀψώνια τῆς ἁμαρτίας [ἐστιν] θάνατος
   "The wages of sin [is] death"
   - ὀψώνια (G3800): military term for soldier's pay/rations; the "earned wages"
   - θάνατος (G2288): Nom Sg M — predicate nominative, "death"
   - sin "pays" death as a wage — earned consequence

2. τὸ χάρισμα τοῦ Θεοῦ [ἐστιν] ζωὴ αἰώνιος ἐν Χριστῷ Ἰησοῦ
   "The gift of God [is] life eternal in Christ Jesus"
   - χάρισμα (G5486): a grace-gift, not earned
   - ζωὴ αἰώνιος: "life eternal" — ζωή (G2222) + αἰώνιος (G166, "age-long/eternal")
   - The contrast: earned wages (death) vs. unearned gift (life)

Key: θάνατος appears as simple predicate — death is what sin earns. The verse
does not qualify what kind of death; context in Rom 5-6 shows it encompasses
both physical death (introduced by Adam) and final separation/second death.

### Grammatical Notes — REV 20:14

Two key clauses:
1. θάνατος καὶ ᾅδης ἐβλήθησαν εἰς τὴν λίμνην τοῦ πυρός
   - ἐβλήθησαν = Aorist Passive Indicative 3rd Plural of βάλλω (ballo, G906)
   - "Death and Hades WERE CAST into the lake of fire"
   - The aorist indicates single completed action (at the end of time)
   - θάνατος (G2288) and ᾅδης (G86) are subjects — personified powers being destroyed

   - Identifying equation: "This [is] the death the second"
   - δεύτερός (G1208): "second" — ordinal adjective attributive position
   - The lake of fire IS the second death

The verse identifies the lake of fire as "the second death" — establishing
ὁ θάνατος ὁ δεύτερος as a technical term in Revelation (see also 2:11; 20:6; 21:8).
The casting of Death and Hades into it means even first-death is ended.

### Grammatical Notes — 1CO 15:26

Very short but theologically dense:
- ἔσχατος (G2078): superlative "last/final" — predicative position before ἐχθρός
- ἐχθρός (G2190): "enemy" — substantival adjective, also Nom Sg M
- καταργεῖται (G2673): Present Passive Indicative — "is being abolished/destroyed/nullified"
  Root: κατά + ἀργός = "down + idle" = to render idle, put out of action, abolish
- ὁ θάνατος (G2288): the subject = "death"

The Greek word order is emphatic: "Last enemy being abolished — [is] death."
καταργέω (G2673) is Paul's key word for total abolition — used for powers
put out of commission. The present tense may be futuristic present or
the process begun in Christ's resurrection that culminates at the parousia.

Cf. v.54: "Death is swallowed up in victory" (quoting Isa 25:8 LXX)
Cf. v.55: "O death, where is your sting?" (quoting Hos 13:14 LXX)

Key phrase: ἵνα διὰ τοῦ θανάτου καταργήσῃ τὸν τὸ κράτος ἔχοντα τοῦ θανάτου

currently under the devil's power/agency, (3) Christ's death destroys the
devil's power over death.

### Grammatical Notes — EPH 2:1

This verse is a participial phrase continued from the end of Eph 1:
"...and what is the exceeding greatness of his power... [2:1] and you,
being dead in trespasses and sins..."

Key grammatical features:
- ὑμᾶς (you) = accusative, object of "he made alive" in v.5
- ὄντας = Pres Act Participle, Acc Pl M — "being" — circumstantial
- νεκροὺς (G3498) = Acc Pl M adjective "dead" — predicate of the participle
  "you, being dead ones"
- τοῖς παραπτώμασιν (G3900) = Dat Pl N — dative of respect/sphere
  "dead IN/WITH RESPECT TO trespasses and sins"
  The dative locates the realm of death — not physical death but moral/spiritual death

The verse does not say "spiritually dead" — it simply says νεκρούς (dead)
in the sphere of transgressions. The word νεκρός is the same word used for
literal corpses — a stark metaphor for moral/relational deadness toward God.

Cf. Col 2:13: "And you, being dead in your sins and the uncircumcision of your flesh..."

### Grammatical Notes — 2TH 1:9

Key terms and their parsing:
- δίκην (G1349): Acc Sg F "penalty/justice/punishment" — the direct object
  δίκην τίνω = idiomatic: "pay the penalty"
- τίσουσιν (G5099): Future Active Indicative 3rd Plural — "they will pay/suffer"
  Certainty of future judgment expressed by future indicative
- ὄλεθρον (G3639): Acc Sg M "destruction/ruin/death" — the content of the penalty
  ὄλεθρος is not "suffering forever" but "destruction" — from ὄλλυμι (to destroy)
- αἰώνιον (G166): Acc Sg M "eternal/age-long" — modifies ὄλεθρον
  "eternal destruction" — note: the destruction is eternal, not the dying
- ἀπὸ προσώπου τοῦ Κυρίου: "from the face/presence of the Lord"
  The ἀπό preposition indicates separation/source — exiled from God's presence

The verse describes the punishment as: ὄλεθρον αἰώνιον = "eternal destruction"
Debate: Does αἰώνιον modify the process (eternal ongoing destruction) or
the result (a destruction that is permanent/irreversible)?
The ἀπό phrase suggests separation from God's presence is the defining feature.
