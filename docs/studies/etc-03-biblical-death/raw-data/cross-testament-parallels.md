## NOTES ON TOOL BEHAVIOR

### OT Book Name Requirements

The cross_testament_parallels_v2.py tool uses LXX/Latin book names for OT references:
- "Isaiah" fails as OT input → use "Jesaia"
- "Genesis" works (English accepted for some books)
- "Job" fails → use "Iob"
- "Psalms" → use "Psalmi"
- "Proverbs" → use "Proverbia"
- "Ezekiel" → use "Ezechiel"
- "Jeremiah" → use "Jeremia"
- "Kings" → use "Reges_I" or "Reges_II"

NT references use standard abbreviations: ROM, REV, 1CO, HEB, EPH, 2TH, etc.

### Score Column Meanings
- Hybrid: combined score (weighted average of semantic + keyword + theological)
- Sem: semantic similarity (vector cosine similarity)
- Key: keyword overlap score
- Theo: theological phrase score (custom phrases; often 0.00 in these results)

### Observations

1. Gen 2:17 OT parallels: strongest connections to Gen 3 (tree of knowledge context)
   and broader wisdom literature on good/evil
2. Gen 2:17 NT parallels: low scores overall — "good/tree" surface matches, not deep
   theological parallels (the tool can't find thematic "death" connections without
   the word appearing explicitly)
3. ROM 6:23 NT parallels: very high scores for Romans 5-8 cluster — confirms
   Paul's concentrated treatment of death/life/Christ in these chapters
4. REV 20:14 OT parallels: mostly fire/burning imagery; no strong OT death-as-lake
   parallels (concept is unique to Revelation in this form)
5. REV 20:14 NT parallels: strongest to other Revelation lake-of-fire passages
   (20:10, 20:13, 21:8) — internal Revelation coherence
6. HEB 2:14 NT parallels: 1JN 3:10 (children of devil) highest — confirms Johannine
   parallel to Hebrews on devil's domain of death
7. ISA 25:8 NT: REV 21:4 and 1CO 15:54 are direct quotation/allusion connections
8. 2TH 1:9 NT: MAT 25:46 parallel confirms semantic link between "eternal destruction"
   and "eternal punishment" language
