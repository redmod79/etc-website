# Topics Research

## Question
What words does the Bible use to describe the fate of the wicked? Examine the primary Hebrew and Greek destruction vocabulary: abad/apollumi (perish/destroy), shamad (destroy/annihilate), kalah (consume/finish), shachath (corruption/destruction), apoleia (destruction/perdition), olethros (destruction/ruin), and related terms. What do these words semantically mean — their lexical definitions, their range of usage, and their grammatical forms in key passages? Do they describe ongoing conscious torment, or cessation of existence/destruction? How does the destruction vocabulary in eschatological contexts relate to the torment vocabulary (basanismos, basanizo)?

## Key Cross-References Discovered in Topic Research

### From WICKED (PEOPLE) — The comprehensive Psalm 37 cluster
The most extensive OT eschatological treatment of the fate of the wicked is Psalm 37, cited multiple times in the PUNISHMENT OF subsection. Key destruction terms in Psalm 37:
- v.2: "shall soon be cut down" (karat metaphor — grass cut)
- v.9: "evildoers shall be cut off" (yikkaretu — Niphal of karat)
- v.10: "the wicked shall not be" (ein rasha — non-existence particle)
- v.20: "the wicked shall perish" (yobedu — Qal of abad); "into smoke shall they consume away" (kalu — kalah)
- v.22: "cursed of him shall be cut off" (karat)
- v.28: "the seed of the wicked shall be cut off" (karat)
- v.34: "when the wicked are cut off" (karat)
- v.38: "the transgressors shall be destroyed together" (nishmadu — Niphal of shamad); "the end of the wicked shall be cut off" (karat)

### Nave's comparison images for the wicked
Nave explicitly groups these similes together, all pointing to consuming destruction rather than preservation in torment:
1. Ashes under the feet (Mal 4:3)
2. Chaff driven by wind (Psa 1:4; Job 21:18; Mat 3:12)
3. Melting wax (Psa 68:2)
4. Smoke dispersing (Hos 13:3; Psa 37:20)
5. Stubble consumed by fire (Job 21:18; Mal 4:1)
6. Morning cloud/dew evaporating (Hos 13:3)
7. Grass withering and cut down (Psa 37:2)

These comparison images uniformly depict consumption, dissolution, and cessation — not ongoing conscious existence in torment.

### The PUNISHMENT ETERNAL list includes both destruction and torment passages
Nave groups under "ETERNAL" (Isa 34:8-10; Dan 12:2; Mat 3:12; 10:28; 18:8; 25:41,46; Mrk 3:29; Luk 3:17; Jhn 5:29; Heb 6:2; 10:28-31; Rev 14:10,11; 19:3; 20:10) — both destruction-vocabulary passages (Mat 10:28 — apollumi; Mal 4:1 destruction imagery) and torment-vocabulary passages (Rev 14:10-11; Rev 20:10) without distinguishing them. The analysis agent must determine whether these represent the same or different outcomes.
