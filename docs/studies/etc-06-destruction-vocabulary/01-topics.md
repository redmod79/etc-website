## Key Observations from Topic Data

1. **The Nave's topic "WICKED, PUNISHMENT OF" is massive** -- over 400 verse references spanning both testaments. This reflects the Bible's extensive treatment of this subject.

2. **The "Compared With" section uses destruction similes exclusively.** The wicked are compared to: ashes (Mal 4:3), chaff (Psa 1:4; Mat 3:12), melting wax (Psa 68:2), smoke (Hos 13:3), stubble (Mal 4:1), morning clouds (Hos 13:3), fuel of fire (Isa 9:19), fading oaks (Isa 1:30), grass (Psa 37:2; 92:7), moth-eaten garments (Isa 50:9; 51:8), passing whirlwinds (Pro 10:25). All of these depict things that are consumed, dissipated, or cease to exist -- none depict ongoing existence in torment.

3. **"PUNISHMENT, ETERNAL" is a relatively short entry** -- 14 verse references. This contrasts with the hundreds of references under "WICKED, PUNISHMENT OF," suggesting the Bible speaks far more about punishment/destruction generally than about its eternal duration.

4. **The FIRE entries link to destruction of the wicked** (Mat 13:42,50; 25:41; Rev 21:8), showing that fire imagery is a subset of the broader destruction vocabulary.
