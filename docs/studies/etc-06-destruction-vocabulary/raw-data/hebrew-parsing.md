**Grammatical notes on יִכָּרֵת֑וּן (yikkaretu):**
- Root: כרת (karat, H3772) — to cut off, cut down
- Stem: Niphal (passive of Qal) — they will be cut off (passive: by God's action)
- Tense: Imperfect (yiqtol) — incomplete action, typically future or habitual
- Person/Number/Gender: 3rd masculine plural — "they (the evildoers) shall be cut off"
- The Niphal of karat is a very strong expression: cut-off implies complete severance, removal, cessation
- Contrast: the waiters on YHWH "shall inherit the earth" — two completely different futures

**Grammatical notes:**
- יֹאבֵ֗דוּ (yobedu): Root H6 (abad), Qal Imperfect 3mp — "they [the wicked] shall perish"
  - Qal stem: basic action, no causative nuance
  - Imperfect: future completion
  - This is the LXX's apollumi — the wicked shall perish (be destroyed/lost)

- כָּל֖וּ (kalu) appears TWICE: Root H3615 (kalah), Qal Perfect 3p — "they are consumed/have been consumed"
  - The simile: "as the fat of lambs: they are consumed; into smoke they are consumed"
  - The doubled kalu intensifies the finality — fully consumed, nothing left
  - Comparison to smoke: smoke dissipates, disappears, leaves nothing behind
  - The fat of lambs: consumed in sacrificial fire, leaving only ash

**Translation analysis of v.20:**
"But the wicked shall perish [abad], and the enemies of the LORD shall be as the fat of lambs: they shall consume [kalah]; into smoke shall they consume away [kalah]."
- Three destruction terms in one verse: abad (perish), kalah (consume), kalah (consume into smoke)
- The image is complete consumption — nothing of the wicked remains, like smoke that vanishes

## Psalm 37:10 — ein (non-existence particle)

**Hebrew Text:** וְעֹ֣וד מְ֭עַט וְאֵ֣ין רָשָׁ֑ע וְהִתְבֹּונַ֖נְתָּ עַל־מְקֹומֹ֣ו וְאֵינֶֽנּוּ׃

| וְ | ו | Conj | and |
| **אֵינֶֽנּוּ** | **אין** | **Noun.ms.Abs+3ms** | **he is not (non-existence)** |

**Grammatical notes on אֵין (ein/ain):**
- אֵין is the Hebrew particle of non-existence — "there is no," "not there"
- Used throughout OT to express absence, non-being, cessation: "there is no wicked person"
- אֵינֶֽנּוּ = "he is not" (ein + 3ms pronominal suffix) — "he is no more"
- The verse says: "yet a little while, and the wicked [shall] not [be]: yea, thou shalt diligently consider his place, and it [shall] not [be]"
- The wicked will be non-existent (ain): you look for him, he is not there
- His very place (location) shall have no trace of him: אֵינֶנּוּ
- This is the most explicit statement of non-existence in the Psalter's wicked-fate passages
- The LXX renders: "καὶ εἰρηνεύσεις ἐν αὐτῷ" — slightly different but the Hebrew text is clear

**Mal 4:1** (Hebrew: כִּי הִנֵּה הַיֹּום בָּא בֹּעֵר כַּתַּנּוּר...):

**Mal 4:3** (Hebrew: וְעַסֹּתֶם רְשָׁעִים כִּי יִהְיוּ אֵפֶר...):
- "ashes" (H665, epher) — what remains when something is completely burned
- epher is the word for "ashes of sacrifice" — the wicked become what the fire leaves behind
- The righteous trample the ashes "under the soles of your feet"
- epher language: complete physical transformation into inert residue

Key conclusion: Mal 4:1-3 presents the wicked as (a) stubble (complete combustion), (b) ashes (post-combustion residue), (c) under the feet of the righteous — a picture of absolute annihilation, not ongoing torment.
