## Textual Variants (N1904 vs TR)

### Chapter-level summary: 2CO 5

### Verse-level comparisons:

**2 Cor 5:1:**
N1904: oIdamen gar hoti ean hE epigeios hEmOn oikia tou skEnous kataluthE, oikodomEn ek Theou echomen, oikian acheiropoiEton aiOnion en tois ouranois.
TR: [word count differs by 3; Strong's in N1904 only: G1437, G2192, G2647, G1492; Strong's in TR only: G5758, G5686, G5719]
Note: Differences appear to be parsing codes not substantive word changes.

**2 Cor 5:3 (CRITICAL VARIANT):**
N1904: ei ge kai endysamenoi ou gymnoi heurethEsometha. (7 words)
TR: eige kai ou gymnoi (4 words)
N1904 has: G1487 (ei), G1065 (ge), G1746 (endyO), G2147 (heuriskO) -- not in TR
TR has: G1489 (eige compound) -- not in N1904
**Note: TR appears shorter; N1904 has the full "having been clothed" (endysamenoi) and "shall be found" (heurethEsometha). This is a significant variant as some MSS read ekdysamenoi ("having been UNclothed") instead of endysamenoi ("having been clothed"). This variant changes the meaning substantially.**

**2 Cor 5:4:**
N1904: 23 words; TR: 21 words
Differences appear to be parsing/morphology codes rather than substantive word changes.

**2 Cor 5:8:**
N1904: 14 words; TR: 12 words
Both contain the key vocabulary (ekdEmEsai, endEmEsai). Differences are minor.
